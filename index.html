    Bio

    I am a research assistant in Duke Computer Vision Lab , working on class imbalance issue in semantic segmentation, advised by Carlo Tomasi. Besides, I have been working as an instructor in Inspirit AI. Before joining Inspirit AI, I received a MS in Computer Engineering from Duke. During my graduate, I interned at AME Lab , advised by Cynthia Rudin and worked on DL & ReRAM project, advised by Krishnendu Chakrabarty .

    I am very interested in: 1) making AI algorithms faster, more generalized and more interpretable with a focus on sparse networks and interpretable algorithms. 2) Class Imbalance Issue in Machine Learning. I recently developed a Leaf-Image-Editor and a Data Augmentation Algorithm to address the class imbalance issue in image semantic segmentation. In the past, I developed FLAME_DB and AHB packages, the first one is to do matching on large scale categorical dataset stored in database and the latter is on both categorical and continuous data.
