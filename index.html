Bio

I am currently working on class imbalance issue in image segmentation at Duke, advised by Carlo Tomasi. Besides, I have been working as an instructor in Inspirit AI to help High and Middle school students to get better understanding of AI. Before joining Inspirit AI, I received a MS in Computer Engineering from Duke. During my graduate, I interned at AME Lab , advised by Cynthia Rudin and worked on DL & ReRAM project, advised by Krishnendu Chakrabarty .

I am very interested in making AI algorithms faster, more generalized and more interpretable. I recently developed a copy/paste insect damages algorithm and a leaves image editor to address the class imbalance issue on semantic image segmentation. In the past, I developed FLAME_DB and AHB, the first one is to do matching on large scale categorical dataset stored in database and the latter is on both categorical and continuous data.
